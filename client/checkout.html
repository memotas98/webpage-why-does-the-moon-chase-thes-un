Why Does the Moon Chase the Sun?

Every week the New York Times comes out with a list of bestselling books and authors and every week the list could be considered an outright lie. Why? Because authors, bookstores, publishers, and agents take advantage of the process. The results are not reflective of the truth. To be clear, these preorders and bulk orders do not represent true organic sales in the marketplace. Very often those who end up on the New York Times Best Seller list do so with staged sales. This is not an honest process. Thus, people with no writing experience become best sellers. And famous people who want more notoriety and those who are unknowns game the system and claim the prized title of a New York Times champion. But is it true? No. Many studies and revelations have been printed about the New York Times Best Seller List and the gig is up. It is a scam, a hollow and shallow marketing scheme of the first order. Meanwhile, authors who write quality works remain obscure and sell little to none of their books. It is time to change this dynamic. It is time for independent authors to earn and receive the recognition they deserve.
